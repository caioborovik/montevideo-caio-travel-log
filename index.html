My Trip to Montevideo

Travel Report - 7th Grade

1. Introduction to the Destination

I traveled to Montevideo, the capital of Uruguay. It is located by the Río de la Plata, which looks almost like an ocean. The city has a mix of old and modern buildings and is famous for its beaches, friendly people, and love for soccer.

The weather is mild most of the year. Montevideo has a lot of history from the colonial period and is known for its traditional music called candombe.

2. Purpose of the Trip

I chose Montevideo because I wanted to visit another South American country and learn more about Uruguayan culture. I also wanted to try local food, see the beaches, and explore the old part of the city.

3. Travel Itinerary

  • Day 1: Flight from São Paulo to Montevideo (2 hours). I stayed in a hotel near the Old Town.
  • Day 2: Walk along the Rambla and lunch at the Mercado del Puerto. I tried the famous chivito.
  • Day 3: Visit to Parque Rodó and the National Museum of Visual Arts. Later, I went to Pocitos Beach.
  • Day 4: Trip to Colonia del Sacramento, a historic town with stone streets.
  • Day 5: Bought souvenirs and watched people drinking mate on the Rambla before flying home.

4. Activities and Experiences

I watched a candombe performance in the streets. People were very friendly, and some even spoke Portuguese with me.

I really liked the peaceful atmosphere of the city. Drinking mate by the water was one of the best moments of the trip.
