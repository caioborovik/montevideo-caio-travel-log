My Trip to Montevideo

Complete Travel Report

1. Introduction to the Destination

Montevideo is the capital of Uruguay, located along the Río de la Plata. The city mixes old colonial architecture with modern buildings and long coastal avenues. It has a mild, temperate climate with warm summers and cool winters. Montevideo is known for its beaches, relaxed atmosphere, and strong cultural traditions such as candombe, an Afro-Uruguayan drum rhythm that is part of the city’s identity.

2. Purpose of the Trip

I chose Montevideo because I wanted to explore another South American country and learn about its culture. I was also interested in trying Uruguayan food, visiting its historic neighborhoods, and seeing how daily life there compares to my own.

3. Itinerary / Travel Route

I traveled by plane from São Paulo to Montevideo. The flight took about two hours. I stayed in a hotel near the Old Town, called Ciudad Vieja.

  • Day 1: Arrival and walk around the historic center.
  • Day 2: Walk along the Rambla, lunch at Mercado del Puerto, and exploring Plaza Independencia.
  • Day 3: Visit to Parque Rodó, an art museum, and a relaxing afternoon at Pocitos Beach.
  • Day 4: Day trip to Colonia del Sacramento, a UNESCO World Heritage site with cobblestone streets and old colonial houses.
  • Day 5: Final morning buying souvenirs and watching locals drink mate by the water before flying home.

4. Activities and Experiences

During the trip, I visited beaches, museums, historic neighborhoods, and parks. I watched a candombe performance on the street, which was one of the most memorable parts of the journey. I also tried traditional foods such as chivito and Uruguayan barbecue. The people I met were friendly, and some even spoke Portuguese with me.

5. Observations on Culture and Customs

Uruguayan culture feels relaxed and welcoming. Many people carry a thermos and a mate cup everywhere they go. The city has a strong culture of walking, socializing outdoors, and enjoying long meals. Compared to my own culture, Montevideo feels more calm, with less hurry in daily life. Still, I noticed similarities in the love for soccer, family gatherings, and music.

6. Personal Reflections and Emotions

Throughout the trip, I felt curious and excited to discover a new place. Watching the sunset at Pocitos Beach made me feel peaceful. The candombe performance surprised me with how powerful and energetic it was. The trip helped me see how different cultures can be while still feeling familiar in some ways.

7. Recommendations for Future Travelers

I recommend visiting the Rambla, especially around sunset. The Mercado del Puerto is great for trying local food, but it can be crowded, so arriving early helps. Colonia del Sacramento is definitely worth a day trip. Comfortable walking shoes are important because the city has many places best explored on foot.

8. Conclusion

Overall, the trip to Montevideo was worth it and gave me new experiences, new foods to try, and new perspectives on culture. I would gladly return someday, especially to explore more beaches and learn more about candombe. The trip reminded me how traveling helps us understand the world through small details and everyday moments.
